Toyota

The first website I found interesting is Toyota South Africa's website(Toyota) The homepage of the website has a lot of videos playing which can be frustrating since I can't stop them, although I can stop the next video from playing and it is clearly labelled which of those videos I'm watching, and I can skip to the one I'd rather watch. What I like about the website is that everything is clearly labelled the homepage has sections that keep you informed on the topic statement. I also like the visuals on the website, they are high definition such that even when scaled down they are still visible and all without sacrificing responsiveness too. I also like that the website goes for a neutral colour scheme with black, white and red so everything is clearly readable. Also, the website has a navigation banner right at the top that clearly indicates which page I am on so I as the user can't get lost.

Now for the things that I find unpleasant, first there's a lot happening on the homepage which can be confusing at times and can make someone forget the point of why they're on the website in the first place. Another thing that I don't really like is that when I navigate away from the homepage to return back to the homepage the button for it is their logo, of which I find confusing because I would never think that a logo would be something to interact with, but I guess looking at it from a business perspective you would want to keep the customer looking at your product catalogue and not an irrelevant home screen.

BBD

Another website I found interesting is bbd software development's website(BBD Software.). What I like about this website is that it is properly structured, each page starts on the top with a background image and a nice bold heading that contrast with the background image and the user is told to scroll down. I also like that there isn't a lot going on in the pages, the contents of that page will be what the heading is about, and all of the paragraphs are center aligned and there is ample whitespaces.

What I don't like about the website however is that there isn't that much feedback to the user on what page they're on. To effectively navigate the site one needs to have very good memory as to not bounce back and forth between two pages you've already visited since the page titles are also unusual, even the page you're on isn't labelled you'll just click and it takes you back to the top of the page.
